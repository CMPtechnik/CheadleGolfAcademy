How Handicaps Are Calculated

Handicaps are calculated by comparing each player's score against the par for the course played. Because there are two course types used in the junior competitions — the Junior Competition (par 32) and the Junior Sixes Competition (par 22) — each round's score is adjusted individually based on the appropriate par.

For each round, the handicap differential is calculated as:

Handicap Differential = (Score - Par) × 0.93

The factor 0.93 is applied to provide a balanced adjustment to the raw score difference.

When players have played rounds on both courses, all their adjusted scores are combined to calculate an overall average handicap. This ensures fairness whether a player competes on the six-hole course or the full nine-hole course.

For example, if a player has two rounds on the six-hole course (par 22) and one round on the nine-hole course (par 32), each round’s score is adjusted individually according to its par, then averaged to produce the final handicap.

This approach allows consistent and fair comparison of players’ performance across different course formats.
